Chuck Noll

One of the greatest NFL head coaches of all time

Notable facts and accomplishments during Chuck's head coaching career:

  • Four-Time Super Bowl Champion-Noll led the Pittsburgh Steelers to four Super Bowl victories (IX, X, XIII, XIV) in the 1970s, making him the first head coach to achieve this feat
  • 23 Seasons as Steelers Head Coach (1969-1991)- The longest-tenured coach in Steelers history at the time
  • Hall of Fame Inductee (1993)- Enshrined in the Pro Football Hall of Fame for his contributions to the game.
  • Turned the Steelers into a Dynasty- Before Noll arrived, the Steelers were one of the worst franchises in the NFL. Under his leadership, they became one of the greatest dynasties in league history
  • Built the Legendary "Steel Curtain" Defense- Developed one of the most dominant defenses in NFL history with players like "Mean" Joe Greene, Jack Lambert, Jack Ham, and Mel Blount
  • First Black Starting Quarterback- Under Noll's coaching, Joe Gilliam became the first African American to start as a quarterback in the NFL during the 1974 season, briefly ahead of Terry Bradshaw.

"Champions are champions not because they do anything extraordinary but because they do the ordinary things better than anyone else."
